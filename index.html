About

Hello! I'm Rendabel "Abel" Radian, originally from Jakarta, Indonesia. At 11, I moved to the U.S., embarking on a journey that led me from a prospective law student to a fervent participant in the tech startup ecosystem. I hold a B.A. in Environmental Science and Sociology from Columbia University, where a spark for innovation transformed my career aspirations.

During my college years, my entrepreneurial spirit thrived through creative ventures, such as crafting smoothies from dorm resources, showcasing my ability to innovate with limited supplies. My professional journey is anchored in product management and research, with a touch of software engineering. Discover the work that fuel my passion here.

As a Co-Founder of LivSync, a tech startup revolutionizing roommate relationships, I've traveled from coast to coast, pitching our vision in venture competitions. Despite our modest beginnings, our team reached the finals in prestigious events like the Columbia Startup Competition in NYC and SageTank in LA, standing out among peers backed by industry giants like Techstars and featured in Forbes.

In my free time, I enjoy playing basketball, biking around New York City, vlogging with my friends, and going to hip-hop/RnB concerts. So if you enjoy any of those, feel free to connect with me!

Work

LivSync | Co-founder

September 2023 - Present

At LivSync, our journey began as a bold bootstrapped venture, swiftly distinguishing ourselves by being named finalists at prestigious competitions like the Columbia Startup Competition and SageTank—achievements not commonly held by self-funded startups. Our innovative approach allowed us to stand shoulder to shoulder with peers featured in Forbes, supported by Techstars, and lauded at Harvard Shark Tank.

Originally conceived as a "Tinder for roommates," LivSync evolved into a pioneering AI-assisted roommate relationship management application. This pivot was not merely a change of direction but a strategic enhancement to meet a real-world need—harmonizing roommate dynamics through technology. Our transformation was fueled by rigorous data analysis and user feedback, which led us to enhance our onboarding flow. This meticulous process improved our LLM-driven user matching accuracy by a staggering 40%, illustrating our commitment to precision and user satisfaction.

Leading a dynamic team of five across diverse functions—engineering, marketing, finance, and product—I implemented semi-weekly standups and robust management practices that increased our productivity by 50%. This streamlined execution was crucial as we navigated the competitive landscape of startup competitions, ensuring that every team member was aligned and focused.

The strategic pivot from a direct-to-consumer (DTC) model to a business-to-business (B2B) framework tripled our annual recurring revenue projections, underscoring our adaptability and business acumen. By prioritizing three key MVP features based on direct user feedback, we not only enhanced user engagement but also addressed the most frequent use cases effectively. Moreover, my hands-on role in designing the UX/UI using Figma ensured that our application was not only functional but also intuitively designed, making complex roommate management simple and engaging.

Projects

Increased Heat Impacts on the Homeless Population in NYC

In a profound exploration of environmental justice, I analyzed nine years of daily temperature data from NOAA's GHCN stations alongside New York City homeless shelter admission records. Utilizing advanced analytical tools such as Excel and R Studio, I uncovered critical heat-related trends that illustrated the heightened vulnerability of NYC’s homeless population to extreme weather conditions. This meticulous data analysis not only shed light on an underreported issue but also paved the way for informed discussions on potential interventions.

To maximize the impact of my research findings, I created a detailed zine that effectively visualized the data. This zine served not only as an educational tool but also as a compelling advocacy instrument, highlighting the potential impacts of these trends on New York City’s healthcare system and policy-making. Through this initiative, I aimed to bridge the gap between data-driven insights and actionable policy changes, contributing to a more informed and responsive urban health policy framework.

Halal Businesses’ Market Adjustments

In an insightful exploration of New York City's cultural and business dynamics post-9/11, I conducted a comprehensive study on Halal businesses, examining how they adapted everything from certification processes to decoration, music, and attire. This study involved a multi-method approach, where I completed eight ethnographies and two in-depth interviews, meticulously integrating both qualitative and quantitative data to provide a holistic view of the sector's evolution.

The core of my research was aimed at understanding the nuanced ways in which these businesses navigated the complexities of cultural identity and regulatory compliance in a post-9/11 context. My findings not only shed light on the resilience and ingenuity of these entrepreneurs but also contributed valuable insights into the broader socio-economic fabric of NYC. This research was further enriched by a thorough review of relevant policies, helping to contextualize the changes within a regulatory framework, thereby offering a comprehensive narrative of adaptation and perseverance.

Creative Thinkers of Tomorrow

Driven by a commitment to educational equity, I founded "Creative Thinkers for Tomorrow," a nonprofit organization dedicated to empowering low-income students in my hometown in New Mexico. Recognizing the diverse needs of these students, I assembled a team of nine dedicated professionals from varied backgrounds, each bringing unique expertise to the table. Together, we launched a comprehensive online mentoring and tutoring program, delivered via Zoom, covering essential academic subjects including math, science, history, and art, as well as physical education.

Our efforts quickly gained recognition, earning a feature in our local newspaper, which highlighted our innovative approach to education and community engagement. To further facilitate access and participation, I designed and built a user-friendly website that not only serves as an informational hub about our services but also streamlines the process for student sign-ups. Through these initiatives, "Creative Thinkers for Tomorrow" has become a beacon of hope and a catalyst for change, providing vital educational support and opening new horizons for the youth in our community.
